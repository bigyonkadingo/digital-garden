MF DOOM is not just an artist, his records transcend the audio medium and inspires the self to reach higher and higher until the world is within their grasp. There is something incredibly unique with DOOM's ability to so masterfully encapsulate and portray the // of a supervillain, almost as if he was acting on the same level of Heath Ledger's Joker and Sir Ian McKellen's Magneto; the impressive edge DOOM has is the limitation of audio, while some may look at the dynamic and eye catching portrayals of villains in movies, DOOM has to play tricks and convince the listener's mind into creating the movie through the process of listening, almost as if he's *controlling their minds.* When you're only visual aspect of your art is a singular album cover, you have no choice but to get creative. 

But it is not just only the reference-filled barrage of beautifully off timed bars that create this movie, but the incredible production and mixing, something that DOOM always has a hand in creating, whether completely solo, or with the joint efforts of legendary producers as Madlib, J Dilla, or Danger Mouse.
